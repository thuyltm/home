Simple breakdown of popular token sale models: ICO, IEO, IDO and STO
1. ICO (Initial Coin Offering)

An ICO is a token sale managed by the project team through a dedicated website or dashboard. It gives complete control over pricing, sales stages, distribution, and community activity. This format suits teams that prefer to launch your own ICO with custom rules and direct access to their audience

2. IEO (Initial Exchange Offering)

An IEO places the token sale in the hands of a centralise exchange. The exchange handles user onboarding, KYC, and the entire sale flow. Investors often trust IEOs because the exchange reviews the project before listing it. 

3. IDO (Initial DEX Offering)

An IDO runs the token sale through a decentralized exchange. It offers quick launch potential and immediate liquidity once trading begins. This model suits projects seeking a community-first launch and fast access to the market, without a lengthy approval step

4. STO (Security Token Offering)

An STO issues tokesn that are subject to securities laws. These tokens may represent ownership rights, revenue share, or asset-backed value. STOs require legal fillings and ongoing compliance, which makes them suitable for regulated sectors such as real estate, equity, and asset-backed products
